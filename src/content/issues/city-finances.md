Smiley entered office in 2022 prioritizing deficit reduction. The debate is whether that discipline was responsible stewardship or austerity that harmed city services.

This issue exposes the cleanest ideological divide in the race. Smiley is a deficit hawk in the mold of Clinton-era Democratic governance. Morales represents the younger left's rejection of that framework, arguing that fiscal restraint is itself a political choice with distributional consequences.

Both framings contain truth. Smiley is right that Providence's structural fiscal position is a real constraint. Morales is right that "fiscal discipline" can be a rhetorical cover for underinvestment in people who depend on public services.

The key analytical question Morales has not answered: where does the money come from? Until his campaign publishes a budget with line-item revenue projections, the anti-austerity platform is a value statement, not a plan.
