Public safety is where the gap between campaign rhetoric and governing reality is widest. Both candidates are talking about a city that is simultaneously under-policed in some neighborhoods (residents report slow response times, open drug markets) and over-policed in others (Black and brown residents face disproportionate stops and enforcement).

Any serious reform agenda has to grapple with that duality. Morales's framing is more intellectually honest about the structural tension. Smiley's approach is more politically durable but risks treating the symptoms.

The key variable neither campaign addresses directly: the police union contract. Whatever reform either candidate promises, the union contract governs what's actually implementable. The next mayoral term includes a contract negotiation. Voters should ask both candidates what they're willing to fight for at the bargaining table.
