Climate is the issue with the longest time horizon and the most severe consequence of inaction — which makes it structurally disadvantaged in an election cycle. Neither candidate has made it central, and the incentives favor talking about rents over sea level rise.

But Providence's climate vulnerabilities are not abstract. Combined sewer overflows are a present problem — heavy rain events flood basements and streets in Olneyville and the West End regularly. Urban heat is measurable and lethal: parts of Providence are significantly hotter than others due to impervious surfaces and lack of tree canopy, and those areas overlap almost perfectly with low-income and minority neighborhoods.

The environmental justice framing Morales uses is analytically correct. The question is whether it translates into capital investment or remains a rhetorical frame.

Smiley's record is maintenance rather than progress. Climate plans exist. Implementation lags. The gap between planning and action is a real accountability question.
