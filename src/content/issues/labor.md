Labor is where Morales's anti-austerity platform has its most concrete application — and its most concrete fiscal test. Raising city worker wages is popular, evidence-based (turnover is expensive), and genuinely within mayoral authority through contract negotiation. It is also not free.

The specific question no one has asked either candidate: what is your wage target for the lowest-paid city workers, what does that cost annually, and what is the revenue source or offset? Without those three answers, a wage increase commitment is a campaign posture, not a policy.

Smiley's record on labor is mixed. Contracts have been negotiated and pension obligations maintained — that is real. But the PPD staffing shortfall (437 officers versus an authorized 528) is partly a compensation competitiveness problem, and three years of fiscal discipline have not solved it.
