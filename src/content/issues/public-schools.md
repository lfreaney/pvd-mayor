Education is the most structurally constrained issue in this race. The mayor has genuine influence — bully pulpit, budget contributions, hiring in city-adjacent roles — but the school system is not theirs to run. Both candidates' platforms bump up against this reality.

Morales's PTU endorsement is meaningful. Teachers know which politicians treat them as partners and which treat them as liabilities. That endorsement reflects something real about how Morales engages with education workers.

Smiley's cooperative approach has not produced visible results after three years, which is a fair basis for criticism. But Morales's confrontational alternative has no clear theory of how it ends — other than hoping the state blinks first.

The honest answer is that improving Providence schools requires sustained state-level political will that neither mayoral candidate can manufacture alone. Voters should ask both candidates: who are your allies in the General Assembly and the Governor's office, and what specific commitments have you secured?
