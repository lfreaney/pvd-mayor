Food security is the issue where the federal-local tension is most acute. Providence has no direct authority over SNAP — it is a federal program administered by the state. What the mayor can do is: maintain emergency food infrastructure, pressure state and federal officials publicly, and build city-level supplemental programs.

Smiley's rhetorical posture is correct — this is genuinely a crisis and naming it as such matters. But rhetoric without a contingency plan is insufficient when 16,000 children are losing benefits.

Morales's structural approach is right in principle but too slow. State-level alternatives to SNAP at scale would take years to build and billions to fund. The people losing benefits today cannot wait.

The honest answer is that Providence needs both: aggressive federal advocacy and rapid expansion of local emergency food infrastructure. Neither candidate has specified what that local infrastructure looks like or what it costs.
