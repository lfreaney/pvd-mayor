  Providence has long-standing sanctuary city policies limiting local law enforcement cooperation with federal immigration authorities (ICE). Under the second Trump administration, these policies face legal and financial pressure — including threats to withhold federal funding from sanctuary jurisdictions. Providence has a large immigrant population, including significant Central American and Cape Verdean communities. The question is not whether to protect residents but how, and at what political and financial cost.

Immigration is the issue where the two candidates are most substantively aligned and the differentiation is mostly performative. Both will maintain sanctuary policies. The question is whether either will invest meaningfully in the affirmative infrastructure — legal funds, community liaisons, know-your-rights programs — that actually changes outcomes for residents.

Smiley's "at war with the federal government" framing was good politics but incomplete policy. Morales's community-trust emphasis is right directionally but uncosted.

The most important question for voters is not which candidate says the right things about immigration — both do — but which has the executive capacity and political capital to actually protect residents when federal pressure escalates. That's a question about governing competence as much as values.
