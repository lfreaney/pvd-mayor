Transportation is the issue where both candidates are weakest on specifics. Morales has the more compelling framing — transit as justice — but no plan. Smiley has the more accurate institutional analysis of where leverage actually sits, but has used that analysis as a reason for passivity rather than advocacy.

The Washington Bridge collapse should have been a galvanizing moment for both campaigns to articulate what Providence's infrastructure relationship with the state should look like. Neither has used it that way. That's a missed opportunity and a data point about how seriously either candidate takes transportation as a policy domain.

One concrete question neither campaign has answered: what is Providence's position on regional transit consolidation or the MBTA's potential expanded presence in the state?
